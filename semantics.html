Penguins are flightless, marine-adapted seabirds (family Spheniscidae) found almost exclusively in the Southern Hemisphere, known for their black-and-white plumage, expert diving abilities, and reliance on fish and krill. With 18 species, they live in diverse habitats ranging from Antarctica to the Galápagos.
